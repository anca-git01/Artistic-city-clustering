Clustering Paris neighbourhoods from an artistic offer point of view

1 Introduction¶

Many people living in Paris are passionate about art, and indeed Paris offers a large variety of artistic activities : art museums, exhibits, theatre plays, operas, concerts. However, the offer is not the same in all the neighbourhoods of Paris. It could be useful to gather neighbourhoods which offer looks alike in clusters for at least two reasons :

  • for people who know they like a neighbourhood and would like to discover other neighbourhoods which offer the same artistic activities.
  • for launching new businesses like music venues or art galleries. The clustering helps them identify
    • the neighbourhoods where the offer is large, which also means that people go to these neighbourhoods because they expect to find these kind of activities.
    • the neighbourhoods where the offer is more limited where it could also be interesting to invest and increase the offer and find new customers.

Hence, this project is aimed to cluster neighbourhhods using criteria related to art. It aims to answering the question « Which neighbourhoods of Paris are similiar from an artistic offer point of view ? »

3 Methodology¶

We will use clustering algorithms in order to find neighbourhoods of Paris which artistic offer looks alike.

First we transform data with a MinMax scaler in order for the variables used to be of equal importance. We perform a KMeans algorithm in order to fit the data.

In order to find the optimal number of clusters we use the Elbow method. As we can see in the following image, the gain in the sum of distances function, decreases more slowly after 6 clusters. Hence, we will perform a KMeans algorithm with 6 clusters.

We can observe that :

  • the first cluster (0), in yellow , is composed of the following neighbourhoods : 10, 11, 13, 20. It is the cluster of neighbourhoods with the highest number of concert halls, street art and a high number of music venues and theatres.

  • the third cluster (2), in green , is composed of two neighbourhoods : 14 and 18. These neighbourhoods present a highest number of theatres, comedy clubs, music venues, and high number of dance studios, and outdoor sculptures and street art.

  • the forth cluster (3), in lightblue , is composed of only one neighbourhood : 1. This neighbourhood is the one of art museums and exhibits, and outdoor scluptures. In this neighbourhood we have the Louvre, Orangerie, Tuileries gardens.

  • the fifth cluster (4), in darkblue , is composed of two neighbourhoods : 9 and 12. These neighbourhoods are the ones where one can find operas and dance studios, and high number of theatres.

  • the sixth cluster (5), in purple , is composed of three neighbourhoods : 3, 4 and 7. These neighbourhoods are the ones where one can find the highest number of art galleries and a high number of art museums.

  • the second cluster (1), in lightgreen , is composed of the other neighbourhoods : 2, 5, 6, 8, 15, 16, 17, 19. These neighbourhoods present an average number of art venues, but no particular concentration in one venue.

5 Discussion¶

Based on the results, people who know they like a neighbourhood ou the neighbourhood they live in, let's say the 11th ring of Paris because of the large offer of concerts, can decide to visit or to live in the 10th, 13th and 20th neighbourhoods of Paris.

An investor who would like to open an art gallery could decide to do so in the 3, 4, 7 neighbourhoods that people visit among other thing because of the high concentration in art galleries, or to develop art galleries in the 9th or 12th neighboourhoods where their number is not high.

However, data used for the study are not exhaustive because of art venues which do not have any associated neighbourhood information in FourSquare. Another study should be done with all the data available.

6 Conclusion¶

This study aimed to identify clusters of Paris neighbourhoods based on their art offer. Different categories of art categories were chosen, mostly art museums, theatres, opera houses, etc. 6 clusters were identified representing neighborhoods with a high number of particular venues in them.

However, cinemas or other types of artistic places, were not included in the study. It would be interesing to see how they would influence clustering. As discussed in the previous section, all art venues did not include information about the neighbourhood they are in. It would thus be of interest to perform the study with those venues in the data set.
